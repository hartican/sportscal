## Scope and source policy

Two scope decisions matter:

1. The user's “round of 18” is best read as **Round of 16**. FIFA's official 2026 knockout bracket names matches 89–96 the Round of 16; there is no Round of 18. Australia were eliminated one stage earlier, in the Round of 32. The requested coverage therefore means every Australia match, then every Round of 16 match onward.
2. Wimbledon publishes a court start and the order of matches, but generally does not publish an exact start time for the second or third match on a court. Those later matches are labelled “follows” below. Do not turn a session start into a false exact match start.

## Wimbledon 2026 — singles, fourth round onward

### Timing convention

Wimbledon was on British Summer Time (UTC+1); Sydney was on AEST (UTC+10), so Sydney was nine hours ahead. The tables use Sydney dates and times.

- `22:00` means a published 1:00 pm No.1 Court start.
- `22:30` means a published 1:30 pm Centre Court start.
- `not before 21:30` or `not before 23:30` is an official lower bound.
- `follows` means Wimbledon supplied match order but no exact start. Do not invent one.

## 2027 Australian Grand Prix — official ticket status

### What is confirmed

- AGPC confirms that the Formula 1 Australian Grand Prix returns to Albert Park in 2027 and invites fans to register for updates: [AGPC register-your-interest page](https://www.grandprix.com.au/tickets/register-your-interest).
- Formula 1's official ticketing page lists Melbourne/Albert Park for the 2027 season but only offers a waitlist: [Formula 1 Australian Grand Prix 2027 ticket page](https://ticketing.formula1.com/australia/).
- AGPC Travel says the 2027 event date is still awaiting confirmation, tickets will be available “soon”, and registration is the way to receive updates: [official AGPC Travel page](https://travel.grandprix.com.au/).

### What is not confirmed

As of the check, no current official source publishes:

- a confirmed 2027 race weekend date;
- a 2027 public ticket-sale date or sale week;
- a 2027 pre-sale week.

Some AGPC product pages currently label stale sale copy as 2027 and say public tickets went on sale in September 2025. That text is internally inconsistent with current 2027 pages: the same product templates mention ticket delivery in early 2026, while the current official registration and travel pages say the 2027 date and sale timing are pending. It is not safe evidence for a 2027 sale-week card.
